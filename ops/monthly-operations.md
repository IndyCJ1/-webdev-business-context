# Monthly Operations

What the business actually does each month once it has paying clients. Solo-operator schedule.

## Weekly (every Monday, 30 min)

- [ ] Check Cloudflare Pages dashboard: any failed deploys?
- [ ] Check uptime monitor: any incidents in the past week?
- [ ] Check Stripe: any failed charges? Send personal email to anyone affected.
- [ ] Check `info@lanierweb.com` and `projects@lanierweb.com` — clear them out
- [ ] Update target list spreadsheet: 10 new leads, 10 new touches

## First Monday of each month (1 hour)

- [ ] Apply included monthly edits per client (per their plan: Starter 1hr, Plus 2hr, Pro 4hr)
- [ ] If they didn't request anything, send them a quick "Anything to update this month?"
- [ ] Run PageSpeed Insights on every active client site — log the score
- [ ] Run uptime report for prior month — log to client's monthly report (Plus/Pro plans)

## Mid-month (15 min)

- [ ] Cloudflare bill check — Pages should be free, Registrar fees show up here
- [ ] Hetzner bill check (once VPS exists) — confirm $7/mo is the only line item
- [ ] Backblaze B2 check (once backups exist)

## End of month (30 min)

- [ ] Stripe → Reports → export CSV of charges for the month
- [ ] Update bookkeeping spreadsheet
- [ ] Tally: how many cold emails sent, replies received, meetings booked, invoices paid
- [ ] If reply rate < 10% on cold emails: rotate templates next month

## Quarterly (2 hours)

- [ ] Re-shoot personal photos / agency-site portfolio if portfolio has changed
- [ ] Review pricing — has demand outstripped capacity? Time to raise prices.
- [ ] Pro plan: schedule 30-min strategy calls with each Pro client
- [ ] Review the website-audit-template.md — is it still accurate? Update if Google's signals have changed.

## Annually (1 day)

- [ ] Renew domains for any client whose registration is at Cloudflare Registrar (auto-renews — verify card is good)
- [ ] Review the service agreement template — anything that bit you in the past 12 months should get clauses fixed
- [ ] Schedule taxes (Schedule C if still sole prop, or LLC return if you've moved up)
- [ ] Take 2 days off. The business can survive it.

## What kills this business

- **Missed monthly edits.** Clients pay for them. If you miss two months in a row, they cancel.
- **Site downtime you don't notice.** If a client tells you their site is down before your monitor does, you've already lost trust.
- **Late replies.** The 24-hour reply promise on the website is a real promise. Set up email rules so client emails never sit unread for a day.
- **Adding too many clients too fast.** With one person, 8–10 active clients is the realistic ceiling. Past that, things slip and the whole thing falls apart.

## What grows this business

- **Saying no to bad-fit clients.** Restaurants who change menus weekly + want hand-holding = 4× the work for the same fee. Pass.
- **Asking for referrals at month 1, 3, and 6** — these are the moments clients are most likely to recommend you.
- **Posting case studies the day a site launches** — momentum compounds.
