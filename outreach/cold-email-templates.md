# Cold Email Templates

Five short emails for cold outreach to local Gainesville-area businesses. Send from `collier@lanierweb.com`. Keep subjects short — they show up truncated on phones.

**Rules:**
- Personalize the first line every time. Reference something specific you saw.
- One CTA per email — never two.
- Never attach anything in a cold email.
- Plain text. No HTML signatures, no logos, no images.
- Five-day gap minimum between emails to the same person.
- Stop after the third email. Move on.

---

## Template 1 — "I noticed your site" (the opener)

**Subject:** quick note about {Business Name}'s site

Hey {First Name},

I drove by {Business Name} last week and the {specific thing — sign / menu / patio} caught my eye, so I looked you up online. The site loads slow on my phone and the menu PDF didn't open at all.

I build websites for local Gainesville businesses — fast, mobile-first, and priced per project (not hourly). Sites usually launch in under three weeks.

Would a free 5-minute audit of what could be improved be useful? No pitch, just the list.

— Collier
Lanier Web · lanierweb.com

## Template 2 — "I made you a mockup" (the hook)

Subject line stays the same on the reply. If they didn't reply to Template 1, escalate by *doing the work*:

**Subject:** mockup for {Business Name}

Hey {First Name},

Followed up on my last email by spending an hour on a homepage mockup for {Business Name} — what it could look like with the real menu and your existing photos.

Live preview: {staging URL}

If you like the direction, I can finish it as a Starter package for $1,500 (one-time) + $50/month for hosting and edits. Three weeks to launch.

If not, the mockup's yours to keep — no obligation.

— Collier
Lanier Web

## Template 3 — "Your competitor just launched" (urgency, only if true)

Use sparingly. Only when actually true.

**Subject:** {Competitor} just redid their site

Hey {First Name},

Saw {Competitor Business} relaunched their website last week. It's working hard for them — clean menu, online ordering, shows up first when I searched "{search term} Gainesville."

I build the same kind of site (just better, honestly). Three-week launch, $1,500 + $50/mo. Want me to put together a quick audit of how {Business Name} compares?

— Collier
Lanier Web

## Template 4 — Referral angle

When you have an existing client to name-drop.

**Subject:** built {Existing Client}'s site — yours next?

Hey {First Name},

I just finished {Existing Client Business}'s new website ({live URL}) — they're a {industry} on {street name} and were dealing with the same issues a lot of small Gainesville businesses have: slow site, hard to update, not showing up on Google.

Now they're getting twice the call volume from the website alone. Took three weeks.

If you're open to it, I'd love to do the same kind of audit for {Business Name}. 5 minutes on Zoom or in person.

— Collier
Lanier Web

## Subject line A/B candidates (rotate to avoid pattern detection)

- "quick note about {Business Name}'s site"
- "{Business Name} on mobile"
- "found {Business Name} on google"
- "5 min for {Business Name}?"

Avoid: "Web design services," "Increase your online presence," anything that screams cold email.

## What to track

For every cold email sent, record in a spreadsheet:

| Date | Business | Contact | Channel | Email # | Replied? | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

When reply rate drops below 10%, the templates need rotation. Currently no real data — start measuring from email #1.
