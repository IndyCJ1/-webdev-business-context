# Target List Workflow

How to find and qualify leads, in a process that survives if you don't do it for two weeks.

## Where to find local businesses with bad/no websites

**Google Maps walks** (highest signal — these are the worst sites)
1. Open Google Maps centered on Gainesville.
2. Search: "{niche} near Gainesville GA" — restaurants, gyms, HVAC, dentists, contractors, barbers, plumbers, salons.
3. Sort by rating descending. **Skip the top 3** — they don't need help.
4. Look at #4–20 — businesses with 4-star ratings and outdated/no websites.
5. Click each. Open the website link. **If it loads in 4+ seconds on your phone, add to list.**

**Drive-by spotting**
- Local strip-mall plazas. Stop and write down names.
- Look for hand-painted signs, old fonts, "Since 1987" — these are the businesses that don't think about their website.

**Facebook over Google**
- If a business's Facebook page has more recent posts than their website's blog, their website is being neglected. They're a lead.

**Chamber of commerce directory**
- gainesvillechamber.com lists members. Worst websites become obvious fast.

## Qualification: which leads are worth pursuing

A lead is **worth a custom mockup** if all three are true:
1. Their current site is genuinely broken (not just ugly — broken: slow, no mobile, no contact info)
2. Their business looks like it does $200K+/year (paying $50/mo is real money to a $50K/year business)
3. You can find a real decision-maker email or walk in and ask for them by name

A lead is **worth a cold email but not custom work** if:
- They look established but have a template site
- You can find an email but not a name

A lead is **not worth pursuing** if:
- They're a chain or franchise (no local decision-maker)
- They have a clean modern site already
- You can't find any contact info at all

## Cadence

| Touch | Day | Channel | Template |
|---|---|---|---|
| First email | Day 0 | email | cold-email-templates.md → Template 1 |
| Mockup follow-up | Day 5 | email | Template 2 |
| Last try | Day 12 | email or walk-in | Template 3 or in-person |
| Done | Day 20 | mark "no response" | move on |

If they reply at any point, drop the cadence and respond to them as a human.

## Weekly minimums (until first paying client)

- 10 new leads added to spreadsheet
- 10 first-touch cold emails sent
- 5 walk-ins per week
- 1 mockup completed for the best lead

That's roughly 4 hours of outreach per week. Below this, the funnel doesn't fill fast enough.

## When to stop cold outreach

- After 3 paying clients, switch to **referral mode** — cold outreach drops to 2 emails per week, walk-ins continue.
- After 5 paying clients, stop cold outreach completely. By then the existing clients should be feeding you 1–2 referrals per quarter.
